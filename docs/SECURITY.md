## Reporting a Vulnerability

We take the security of our infinite-context and cryptographic implementations seriously. If you believe you have found a vulnerability, specifically related to **Entropy Collapse** in the QRT damping logic or **Subgroup Isolation** in the TUPT signature scheme, please follow the protocol below.

### Disclosure Protocol

1.  **Direct Communication**: Email a detailed technical description to `security@nexusresonance.com`.
2.  **Encryption**: We recommend using PGP to encrypt your report. The public key is available on our official website.
3.  **No Public Disclosure**: Please refrain from disclosing the vulnerability publicly until we have reached a resolution. We aim to acknowledge all reports within 48 hours and provide a fix or mitigation within 14 days.

### Exclusions
- Vulnerabilities introduced by users utilizing chaotic 3-6-9 attractor scalars in their own independent implementations are outside our support scope.

## Vulnerability Response

The project team coordinates with security researchers to address identified issues through a responsible disclosure process. We prioritize stability and cryptographic integrity across all supported versions.

*Thank you for helping us maintain the absolute integrity of discrete computational boundaries.*
